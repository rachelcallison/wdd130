Client/Developer Team Project - Report

Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

When planning the content for my site, I focused on selecting information and visuals that would clearly convey the theme and purpose of the project. I searched for stock images that fit the overall aesthetic and tone I wanted the site to have. I also decided for the two sub pages to do one about the history of space exploration and another about the solar system. These topics seemed to best represent the type of content I wanted to share, and organizing the site this way allowed visitors to easily navigate between educational content and visual illustrations. Overall, my goal was to create a site that was informative, visually appealing, and easy to understand.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

Communication with the developer happened through Microsoft Teams, but it was not very frequent. While I was able to answer a few of their questions and provide some guidance when asked, the limited interactions made it difficult to keep the project on track. For example, I never received a link to view the site while it was in progress, I knew the lead's GitHub ID so I was able to guess the url. This experience emphasized the importance of consistent and proactive communication when working with a developer or team on a project.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

Not entirely. While the developers did complete the work, the site did not fully reflect my vision; I was able to see the home page and the solar system page but I naver saw the History page. Certain elements I expected to see were missing or implemented differently than I had imagined. This experience was a learning opportunity for me, as it emphasized the importance of setting clear expectations from the start and regularly checking in to ensure the project is aligned with those goals.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

In the future, I will make sure to follow up more consistently and establish a structured communication plan with the team. I would also request regular progress updates, previews of the site, and clear milestones to ensure that the project is progressing according to my expectations. This would help prevent miscommunication and ensure that both the client and the development team are on the same page throughout the entire process.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

The most challenging part of this project was dealing with a lack of communication from the client/team lead. This made it difficult to get the information and files needed to complete the site as intended. While I wasn’t able to fully overcome this challenge, I focused on creating two pages for the site where the client was the lead, which allowed me to still contribute significantly to the project and complete three pages in total.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

I enjoyed the opportunity to work on someone else’s idea and try to bring it to life in a way that matched their vision. It was interesting to see how different perspectives influenced the design and content decisions, and it challenged me to be creative while still adhering to the client’s guidelines. This experience provided valuable insight into collaborative web development and project planning.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

If I could do the project again, I would set clearer deadlines and checkpoints with both the client and the development team. This would help ensure that all necessary content is received on time and that feedback is provided promptly. Establishing a structured communication plan from the beginning would likely improve efficiency and help avoid misunderstandings.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

Yes. This project gave me practical experience in managing web development tasks, working with clients, and collaborating with team members. It taught me how to handle challenges such as delayed communication and unclear expectations, and how to adapt to ensure that progress continues. Overall, I feel more prepared and confident to take on similar real-world projects in the future.
